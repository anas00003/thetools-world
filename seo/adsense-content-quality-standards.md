# TheTools.World AdSense Content Quality Standards

Production domain: https://thetools.world

These standards keep TheTools.World useful for visitors and safer for AdSense review. They are not word-count targets. A page should earn its place by solving a clear task with accurate, original, page-specific guidance.

## Tool Pages

Each tool page should include:

- A direct explanation of what the tool does.
- Who should use it and when it is useful.
- Step-by-step usage instructions.
- Supported inputs and outputs where true.
- Practical limitations, especially file-size, format, quality, and browser-support limits.
- Browser-side/privacy wording where the workflow uses browser APIs.
- Troubleshooting for common failure cases.
- Related tools that naturally help with the same workflow.
- FAQ when it answers real user questions.
- No fake guarantees, fake popularity claims, reviews, ratings, awards, or ad-click language.

## Exact-Size Compression Pages

Each exact-size compression page should include:

- The target-size purpose and likely upload context.
- A realistic note that exact output is not assured for every image.
- Why that size is used and when a larger or smaller target may be better.
- How to get closer to the target using width and quality.
- What to do if output is still too large.
- Image quality, face clarity, document readability, or screenshot readability warnings.
- Use cases tied to the specific target size.
- Related sizes and related compressor/resizer tools.
- FAQ that is visible and matched by FAQPage schema.
- Unique copy per size, especially for 20KB versus 200KB pages.

## Converter Pages

Each converter page should include:

- What the conversion means.
- When to use the output format.
- What the conversion cannot do.
- Format limitations, including quality, transparency, and background behavior where relevant.
- Step-by-step usage.
- Troubleshooting.
- Related converter, compressor, and resizer tools.
- FAQ with matching FAQPage schema when FAQ is present.

## Form And Photo Utility Pages

Each form or photo utility page should include:

- The practical upload problem it helps solve.
- Common form, portal, profile, or document situations.
- Step-by-step usage.
- Size, dimension, format, and readability guidance.
- Privacy/browser-side note where true.
- Limits stated carefully, especially no promise of official approval.
- Related compressor, resizer, exact-size, and passport/photo tools.
- FAQ where users may confuse preparation with official compliance.

## Support And Legal Pages

Support and legal pages should include:

- A clear purpose.
- Professional, plain-language wording.
- Enough detail for trust without over-optimization.
- Accurate analytics, cookies, advertising, privacy, contact, and user-responsibility language.
- No fake company, team, legal-compliance, review, rating, or traffic claims.
- Links to important pages only where useful.

## Homepage And Hub Pages

Homepage and hub pages should include:

- What the site or category offers.
- Useful grouping of tools.
- Common tasks users can solve.
- A simple "start here" path.
- Browser-side/privacy positioning where true.
- Links to major tools and category pages.
- No generic filler, fake trust signals, or repetitive keyword phrases.

## Tone Rules

Use clear, practical, honest language. Prefer short paragraphs, natural headings, and real task examples.

Avoid:

- "In today's digital world"
- "ultimate solution"
- "seamlessly revolutionize"
- "best tool"
- "trusted by millions"
- "guaranteed"
- "100% perfect"
- "AI-powered" unless there is a real AI workflow
- Repeated exact-match SEO phrases

## Schema Rules

- Use FAQPage only when the FAQ is visible on the page.
- FAQPage questions and answers must exactly match visible FAQ text.
- Do not add reviews, ratings, aggregateRating, fake sameAs links, or fake organization details.
- Keep Organization and WebSite schema accurate and limited to supported facts.
