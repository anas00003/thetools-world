# TheTools.World Keyword Research Process

Use this process before adding any new indexable page.

## Data Sources

- Google Keyword Planner: export volume ranges, competition, bid ranges, and keyword variants.
- Google Trends: compare seasonality, region demand, and rising queries.
- Google autocomplete: collect exact long-tail phrases from the search box.
- People Also Ask: collect recurring user questions that can become FAQ or guide sections.
- Ahrefs free keyword tools: collect difficulty, related questions, and SERP ideas.
- Search Console after launch: use real impressions, clicks, CTR, average position, and query/page pairs.
- Competitor pages: collect title, H1, content angle, related tools, ad patterns, and schema.

## Keyword Scoring Model

Score each factor from 1 to 5. Higher is better except competition difficulty, where 5 means easier to compete.

| Factor | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Search intent clarity | Vague or mixed | Some tool intent | Clear tool/action intent |
| Traffic potential | Very low | Moderate | Strong volume or clear demand |
| Competition difficulty | Dominated by giants | Mixed SERP | Realistic opportunity |
| Monetization value | Low ad value | General utility value | Strong commercial/AdSense fit |
| Tool feasibility | Hard or risky | Moderate complexity | Can build safely in browser |
| Content uniqueness | Commodity only | Some distinct angle | Strong unique value or workflow |
| Internal linking fit | Isolated | Some related links | Fits existing clusters well |
| AdSense safety | Risky or sensitive | Needs careful wording | Safe utility intent |

## Decision Classes

- Build now: high intent, feasible, distinct, safe, and useful.
- Build later: promising but needs more data, design, or implementation work.
- Merge into existing page: similar intent already covered by an existing page.
- Avoid: thin, risky, misleading, too competitive, or not a real tool opportunity.

## Minimum Data Before Build

- Primary keyword and 5 to 20 secondary variants.
- Top 10 ranking URLs and notes.
- Distinct user intent statement.
- Tool behavior requirement.
- Content outline.
- Related internal links.
- Schema recommendation.
- AdSense and trust risk notes.
